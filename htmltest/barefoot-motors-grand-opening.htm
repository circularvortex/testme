Barefoot Motors Grand Opening

  • View
  • Edit

  • Dev load
  • Dev render
Posted by jeremy on Sep 18, 2009 | Edit

A few of us from Arcimoto made the trek down to Ashland, OR last Thursday (June 25th) for the Grand Opening of Barefoot Motors (http://www.barefootmotors.com). In case you haven’t heard of them, Barefoot makes an electric ATV that they call an EUV (earth utility vehicle). The event was attended by fellow EV industry gurus, city council members, and local folk from in and around Ashland. Max Jr, son of CEO Max Scheder, gave brief safety tips and instructions before handing over the keys to one of Barefoot’s new EUVs. The test drive course was a small loop on a bumpy dirt path with a variety of hills, bumps, bends, and curves.
Joe & Max Jr.

Joe & Max Jr.

Helmet on, I climbed onto the warm black seat of the EUV and turned the power on. I sat for a moment, examining the handlebars, brake, and throttle. Something was missing but I couldn’t put my finger on what it was. With a strong push of the throttle, the vehicle accelerated quickly and quietly, leaving a sunlit cloud of dust in its place. I took it easy at first, unsure of the power of the vehicle beneath me, but by the end of the ride I was accelerating up hills and around corners. The vehicle handled well and as I came around the final corner and drove the EUV back to its original location, I figure out what was missing: the sleek electric motor failed to produce the loud and unpleasant noises I’ve been conditioned to expect from an ATV. Quiet, comfortable, efficient, sexy. Good job Barefoot.
